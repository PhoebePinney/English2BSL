ABOUT

What this app can do

This web app aims to translate sentences in written English into a video showing British Sign Language. Use the translation tool to quickly find out the sign language translation for whatever it is you want to communicate. If you're hoping to broaden your vocabulary of signs, develop BSL as a new skill or improve your communication skills with sign users, this app can be a powerful learning tool for you!

Why this app was made

The British Deaf Association states that around 151,000 people in the UK are BSL users, with over 87,000 having BSL as a first language over English. Yet despite this, BSL is not widely learnt among hearing people which can often lead to Deaf communities feeling isolated or ignored. This tool was made with the aim to bridge the communication barrier between BSL users as well as encouraging the learning of BSL.

Notes on the app

  • It is important to note that BSL is a very expressive language, with a lot of meaning conveyed through facial expressions, mouth movements and body language. This translation tool can demonstrate the hand shapes used within BSL, but cannot fully capture the emotion behind each word. When signing, remember to mouth words as you sign them and convey as much emotion as you can - for example, have a quizzical expression when asking a question.
  • This tool has a limited but ever-growing dictionary of signs; words not in the sign dictionary will be spelt out letter-by-letter. However, the grammatical struture given is accurate to BSL and gives a good overview of the language.
